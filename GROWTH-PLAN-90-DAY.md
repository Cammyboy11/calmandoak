## Pinterest — your fast traffic engine (do this from day 1)

Pinterest can drive real visitors in weeks while Google warms up. Home/Japandi is one of the strongest niches on the platform, and you already have boards, templates, and Rich Pins enabled.

- [ ] **5–10 fresh pins per day** (new designs, not repins). Batch-create weekly, schedule with Tailwind or the native scheduler.
- [ ] **Multiple distinct pins per URL** — 3–5 different images for each important page (especially the office, colours, and print pages).
- [ ] **Prioritise pinning your high-impression pages**: office/workspace, colour palette, prints, Looks.
- [ ] **2–4 Idea Pins per week** (native, stays on platform, boosts distribution).
- [ ] **Seasonal pins 6–8 weeks early** — start fall pins in August, holiday in October.
- [ ] Keyword-optimise pin titles + descriptions like mini blog posts (Pinterest is a search engine).
- [ ] Track which pins drive clicks in Pinterest Analytics; make more of what works.

**Target:** 100+ fresh pins/month, growing monthly viewers steadily.

## Email — the audience you actually own

Algorithms will betray you eventually; the list won't. Your starter-guide lead magnet is the right hook.

## Backlinks & authority — the lever that separates plateaus from breakouts

Most decor sites stall here. A handful of real links moves entire clusters.

- [ ] **HARO / Qwoted / Featured.com** — respond to 3 home/interiors journalist queries per week with a short expert quote. This is the highest-ROI way to earn links from real publications.
- [ ] **2–3 guest posts** on home/decor blogs over the 90 days (link back to a cluster hub, not the homepage).
- [ ] **Pitch your link-magnet assets** (the 2026 trend report + the palette cheat-sheet) to roundups and "best Japandi blogs" lists.
- [ ] **Reclaim mentions** — search for sites mentioning "Calm & Oak" or using your content without linking, and ask for a link.
- [ ] Keep the **internal-link mesh** healthy as you publish (every new post links to 3–5 relevant existing pages).

**Target:** 3–5 quality backlinks by day 90.

## The realistic arc (so you don't quit at the wrong moment)

- **Months 1–3 (this plan):** Pinterest carries early traffic; you build the content base; Google starts indexing the backlog and the office cluster climbs.
- **Months 4–12:** SEO compounds — clusters rank together, email grows, backlinks lift authority. Traffic curve starts bending upward.
- **Month 12–18+:** cross the display-ad threshold (Mediavine ~50k sessions/mo, Raptive ~100k pageviews/mo — home/lifestyle has some of the highest ad RPMs), and your own prints + email carry the margin.

Most people quit in month 2–3 — exactly when Pinterest is working and Google is about to turn on. The single biggest predictor of success is simply continuing to publish.
