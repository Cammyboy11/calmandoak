# Calm & Oak — Etsy About-Page Video Shoot Brief

A production-grade iPhone shoot you can execute in 45 minutes. Output: a 15-second silent vertical MP4 that reads as a real studio, not a stock-footage compile.

The brief is more demanding than your average phone shoot because the alternative is the slideshow placeholder, and that slideshow looks like a slideshow. This shoot will not.

---

## 0. Why this matters

Etsy About videos autoplay muted on the page. They run 5–15 seconds, on loop. They are the single piece of moving content a buyer sees on your shop. Top Japandi shops use them to communicate three things in silence:

1. **A person is actually behind this.** Not a dropship.
2. **The materials are real.** Paper, ink, hands, light.
3. **The aesthetic is consistent.** What's in the video matches what's in the prints.

If you nail those three, the buyer trusts the price tag on the 16×20.

---

## 1. Pre-shoot — gather this in 10 minutes

### Location
A table with a window to one side. North-facing or east-facing windows give the softest light. Avoid:
- Direct midday sun (harsh)
- Overhead room lights (flatten everything)
- Any visible LED bulb in frame (color cast)

The table can be your kitchen table, your desk, or a board on two sawhorses. What matters is the light coming from **one direction** — the side, never the front.

### Surface
Pale oak, ash, or birch is the brand. If your table is dark, drape a piece of unbleached linen, kraft paper, or a pale wood cutting board across the shooting area (60×60 cm is enough).

### Props (gather these — most are in your home)
| Prop | Why | Substitute |
|---|---|---|
| One sheet of warm off-white paper | The print stand-in | Any 8×10 cream paper, or one of your actual print masters |
| A small inkwell or shallow ceramic dish | The "ink" pour | An espresso cup with dark soy sauce or strong black tea |
| A brush — Japanese sumi-e brush ideal | The action prop | Any soft-tipped paintbrush, calligraphy brush, or even a square chisel watercolor brush |
| One dried branch — eucalyptus, olive, or wild grass | Negative-space prop | Any single dried stem |
| One small ceramic vase or stoneware bowl | Anchor object | A small mug, a hand-thrown cup, anything in matte off-white or cream |
| One finished print + light oak frame | The hero shot | If you don't have a frame, lean an unframed print against a wall |
| A clean kraft mailer tube OR flat mailer | Packaging shot | Any plain brown mailing tube or large kraft envelope |
| A neutral linen cloth or napkin | Texture detail | Any cream/oat-colored fabric |

**Brand colors** for prop selection: cream `#F7F4EE`, warm cream `#EFE8DA`, charcoal `#2A2A28`, oak tones, one terracotta accent allowed (a ceramic, a thread, a ribbon).

**What NOT to put in frame:** anything plastic, anything glossy, anything in pure white, anything with a logo, your phone, your laptop, modern overhead lighting, fake plants, words other than the print itself, faces, hands above the wrist (forearms cut clean by the frame).

### iPhone setup
- **Camera app**, NOT third-party. Use the native one.
- **Mode:** Video, not Cinematic. Cinematic adds fake bokeh that breaks the natural feel.
- **Resolution:** 4K at 30 fps (Settings → Camera → Record Video → 4K at 30 fps). **NOT 60 fps** — 60 fps reads as iPhone-handheld; 30 fps reads as cinematic.
- **HDR Video:** OFF (Settings → Camera → Record Video → HDR Video toggle off). HDR videos look blown out on Etsy's player.
- **Lock exposure + focus on every shot.** Long-press in the viewfinder until "AE/AF LOCK" appears at the top.
- **Tripod / stable surface.** Phone stand, book stack, anything that holds it still. **No handheld.** Movement should come from the subject, not the camera.

## 2. Shot list — 10 shots, total ~50 seconds raw, edits to 15 sec

Each shot is 3–5 seconds **held still on the tripod**. Don't pan. Don't tilt. Don't zoom. Static beats motion every time for this aesthetic.

Shoot all ten — then cut down in post.

| # | Shot | Duration raw | Description | What it sells |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | **Window light & paper** | 5 sec | Top-down on a single sheet of cream paper, empty. Soft window light from camera-left, drawing one shadow line across the page. | "This is a quiet, considered space." |
| **2** | **Ink bowl** | 4 sec | Tight overhead on the ink bowl — just the dark surface, the rim, the cream surface around it. Maybe a single ripple. | Material detail. |
| **3** | **The brush** | 4 sec | Overhead — your hand (wrist down only) picks up the brush, dips it into the ink, holds for a beat. **One slow motion.** | The maker is human. |
| **4** | **The mark** | 5 sec | Overhead — slow, deliberate single brushstroke across the cream paper. Hold for 2 sec after the stroke lands. | Original work. |
| **5** | **Ink + branch** | 4 sec | Flat-lay: paper with brushstroke from shot 4, brush laid alongside, dried branch curving in from the corner, ink bowl at edge. Static, no movement. | The whole aesthetic in one frame. |
| **6** | **Finished print on table** | 5 sec | The hero shot. A finished, framed print propped against a wall or leaning on the table. Side light. Hold steady — let the eye travel. | The product is the result. |
| **7** | **Detail — paper edge** | 4 sec | Macro angle on the corner of a print: the paper texture, the ink edge where it meets the cream. Raking side-light. | "250 gsm, fine-art paper" without words. |
| **8** | **Packaging** | 5 sec | Hand sliding a rolled or flat print into a kraft mailer / tube. One slow motion. The brand stamp visible if you have one (or a simple "Calm & Oak" label). | The product arrives in the mail. |
| **9** | **Hand placing on wall** | 5 sec | Wide shot — a hand holding a framed print up against a warm-white wall, finding the right spot. Hold the print in the final position for 2 sec. | The buyer sees themselves. |
| **10** | **Final gallery wall** | 5 sec | The wide closer. Three framed prints in a gallery wall arrangement on a wall, soft natural light. The room shows just a hint — a corner of an oak shelf, a branch in a vase off to the side. Hold steady. | The end state. |

### Shot order on the day

Shoot **1–4 in one window-light setup** (overhead on table, props laid). 10 minutes.
Shoot **5–8 in same setup** with prop changes. 10 minutes.
Move setup for **9–10** (against a wall, different framing). 10 minutes.
Buffer for retakes. 15 minutes.

Total: ~45 minutes if your house is somewhat ready.

## 3. Pacing & motion rules

These are what separate a Kinfolk-feeling video from a TikTok-feeling video:

- **Hold every shot 1 full second longer than feels right.** Your instinct will be too fast. Resist it.
- **No transitions.** Hard cuts only. No fades, no wipes, no dissolves — Etsy's player can't handle them anyway and they look amateur.
- **One motion per shot.** Either the camera moves, the subject moves, or *nothing* moves. Never two.
- **Use stillness as a beat.** A 3-second hold on an empty paper is fine. Resist the urge to fill it.
- **No music.** Etsy autoplays muted. Don't add a soundtrack that won't play. (You can add ambient room sound for IG / Pinterest later — see Section 6.)

## 4. Edit — 10 minutes in iMovie or iPhone Photos

iPhone Photos > Albums > Videos > select the 10 shots > select all > Share > Save to Files (transfer to laptop) OR edit on phone in iMovie.

### iMovie sequence (15 sec total)

| Time | Clip | Trim to |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00–0:02 | Shot 1 (paper + light) | 2 sec |
| 0:02–0:04 | Shot 3 (hand + brush dip) | 2 sec |
| 0:04–0:06 | Shot 4 (the mark) | 2 sec |
| 0:06–0:08 | Shot 5 (flat-lay) | 2 sec |
| 0:08–0:10 | Shot 6 (finished print) | 2 sec |
| 0:10–0:12 | Shot 8 (packaging) | 2 sec |
| 0:12–0:14 | Shot 9 (placing on wall) | 2 sec |
| 0:14–0:15 | Shot 10 (gallery wall, last frame holds) | 1 sec |

### iMovie color settings
- Apply the **"Original"** look — no preset filter. Etsy's player crushes already-graded video.
- If exposure varies between shots, use "Auto" in the Adjust menu on each clip individually — don't apply globally.
- **No text overlays.** The product, not the words.

### Export
- **Format:** MP4 (iMovie default)
- **Resolution:** 1080p (NOT 4K — Etsy's About player caps at 1080)
- **Frame rate:** 30 fps (will match your shoot setting automatically)
- **File size target:** under 50 MB (Etsy max is 100 MB, but smaller loads faster)

---

## 5. Quality checklist — before you upload

Run through this. If any item fails, reshoot that single shot. Don't accept anything that fails.

- [ ] Every shot is sharp — no motion blur
- [ ] Every shot has soft directional light, not flat overhead
- [ ] No artificial lighting visible in any frame
- [ ] No hands above the wrist
- [ ] No words on screen
- [ ] No glossy or plastic objects in frame
- [ ] Colors are warm/cream, no harsh whites
- [ ] No clip is shorter than 1 sec or longer than 3 sec (loses attention)
- [ ] Final clip is 12–15 sec total
- [ ] File is under 50 MB
- [ ] The video loops cleanly — the last frame doesn't feel cut off

---

## 6. Optional: 60-sec version for Instagram / Pinterest

If you're going to shoot anyway, capture an extra take of **shot 4** and **shot 9** at full duration (let the brushstroke take its full 6 seconds; let the hand placing the print on the wall take its full 8 seconds). 

You can edit a separate 60-sec long-form video later for:
- **Instagram Reels** (9:16, 60 sec, with quiet ambient sound — paper rustle, brush on paper)
- **Pinterest Idea Pins** (9:16, 60 sec)
- **TikTok** if you want — same format

That single shoot day produces both the Etsy 15-sec silent version AND the long-form social version. Same setup, same props, same 45 minutes.

---

## 7. If a shot fails — substitution rules

| If you can't get | Use instead |
|---|---|
| A sumi-e brush | Calligraphy brush, watercolor round brush size 12+, or even a clean Q-tip on cream paper for the "mark" shot — it's about the gesture |
| Real ink | Black tea concentrated 3× in espresso, or food-grade activated charcoal mixed with water, or instant coffee at 4×-strength |
| An oak frame | Lean an unframed print against a wall, prop with a small ceramic — the unframed look is on-brand anyway |
| A dried branch | A single cut stem from any houseplant (eucalyptus stem from a grocery bouquet is $4) |
| A clean wall | A leaning bookshelf with very few books, or a corner of a blank wall in any room |
| All ten shots | Cut to the 5 strongest. A 10-second video that's all strong shots beats a 15-second video with two weak ones. |

## 8. Replace the placeholder slideshow

The current `assets/img/etsy-brand/about-video.mp4` is the placeholder. When your new video is ready, save it to the **same path** (`assets/img/etsy-brand/about-video.mp4`) and overwrite. The Etsy About-page video field is uploaded directly via the dashboard — there's no link, so once you upload the new one, it replaces the placeholder.

---

## TL;DR — the 45-minute version

1. **Find** a table by a window, around morning or afternoon. 5 min.
2. **Set up** props per Section 1 (paper, ink, brush, branch, ceramic, frame, mailer). 10 min.
3. **Lock the iPhone** to 4K 30fps, HDR off, mount on tripod or book stack. 2 min.
4. **Shoot the 10 shots** in Section 2, holding each one 3–5 sec, static camera. 25 min.
5. **Edit** in iMovie to 15 sec per Section 4. 10 min.
6. **Check** quality per Section 5. 3 min.
7. **Upload** to Etsy About page, replacing the current placeholder. 1 min.

Total: 56 min. Beats the slideshow by every measure that matters.
