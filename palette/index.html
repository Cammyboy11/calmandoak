How a Japandi palette is built

Three rules, five colours

A warm base

Start with a warm neutral — oat, cream, or stone — never a cool blue-white. It sets the whole room's temperature.

Two woods, no more

One light wood (oak, ash) and at most one dark (walnut). A third wood tone is where rooms start to feel busy.

One quiet accent

A single restrained note — terracotta, sage, or charcoal — used once or twice. One accent keeps it calm; two make it loud.
