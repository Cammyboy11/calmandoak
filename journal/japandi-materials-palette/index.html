Japandi materials & palette

Japandi is a materials palette before it's a colour scheme. Get the woods, the fibres, and the clay right, and the colours mostly take care of themselves. Here's the whole material world in one place.

Two of the five Japandi principles are really about materials: "two woods, never more" and "things that age well." This guide pulls the whole material palette together — what to use, and the colours that come with it.

The materials

  • Wood — the backbone. Solid oak and ash for the light pieces, walnut for the dark accents. Real wood over veneer and painted MDF; it's the difference between a room that ages and one that wears out (see honest materials).
  • Linen, wool & cotton — the softness. Washed linen for bedding and curtains, wool for throws and rugs, cotton for the everyday. Natural fibres breathe and improve; here's when to use each.
  • Stoneware & ceramic — the handmade note. Matte, speckled, slightly imperfect — the heart of wabi-sabi.
  • Paper, rattan & jute — the light-and-shadow layer. Paper lampshades, woven baskets, a jute rug. They diffuse light and add texture without adding colour.
  • Metal, sparingly — real brass or blackened steel for lighting and hardware. A little, never shiny.

The two-woods rule

The fastest way to keep a material palette calm: choose two woods and commit — a lighter one for the big pieces, a darker one for the accents. A third wood reads as inventory. The full logic is in the two-woods rule.

The colour palette

Japandi colour is really four roles:

  • Base: warm white, oat, soft cream — walls and big upholstery.
  • Wood: oak (light) and walnut (dark) — the two-wood pairing above.
  • Accent: one muted colour — sage, charcoal, or terracotta. Pick one.
  • Texture: stoneware, paper, linen, jute — the "colour" that's actually material.

For five combinations that always work — oat-on-oak, charcoal-on-cream, sage-and-stone — see the colour palette guide. And remember: layer three textile weights in one colour family so the room reads rich, not flat.
