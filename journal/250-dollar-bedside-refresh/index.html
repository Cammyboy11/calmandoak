The $250 bedside refresh

If the full Japandi bedroom is too much budget at once, start here. Five small pieces under $250 transform just the nightstand surface — the most photographed corner of any bedroom.

The five pieces

1. The lamp pair — ~$60

A pair of small ceramic bedside lamps. The PoKat Small Ceramic Lamp Set 20.75" (4.6★/767 reviews) on Lighting. Buying the pair (instead of two singles) makes both nightstands match instantly.

2. The water carafe + tumbler — ~$35

A stoneware or glass water carafe with a tumbler that doubles as the lid. Replaces "the plastic glass on the nightstand" with something you actually want to look at. Browse Ceramics & Tableware.

3. The small ring dish — ~$15

A 3-inch matte stoneware ring dish for jewelry, watch, the small things. The bedside catch-all that prevents the surface from collecting clutter.

4. The framed art (single piece) — ~$60

One framed sumi-e ink print or botanical print, 8x10 or 11x14, in a wood or matte black frame. Hung above the nightstand or leaning against the wall.

5. The wood alarm clock — ~$30

Small wooden bedside alarm clock with soft warm display. Replaces the phone-as-alarm-clock habit. The room-changing piece — a real alarm clock means the phone lives in the kitchen, the bedroom becomes a phone-free zone.

Total: $200. The remaining $50 of budget covers a single dried branch in a small ceramic vase ($15) and a folded linen runner across the dresser top ($35).
