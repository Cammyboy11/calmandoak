The $300 Japandi entryway

The entryway is the first room your guests see and the last room you photograph for your real estate listing. It's also the room most homes treat as an afterthought — a pile of shoes by the door, a coat rack overflowing, mail on every surface. Three hundred dollars and one weekend can fix that completely.

The five pieces that make the room

An entryway only needs five things to feel finished:

  1. A narrow console table
  2. A basket below it (for shoes)
  3. A small lamp on it (warm bulb, dimmer)
  4. A mirror above it
  5. A tray on the surface (for keys + the small things)

That's it. Add a single dried branch in a small vase if you have ten extra dollars. Skip the umbrella stand, skip the wall hooks for coats (those go behind a door), skip the rug runner (tile or wood floor reads cleaner). Five pieces.

The build, piece by piece

1. The console — ~$130

Slim, oak, two-tier (drawer + open shelf below). The depth matters most: 12-14 inches, no deeper. Anything wider eats the entryway and forces guests to walk around it. Browse the verified pick on our Furniture pillar — same Besiost console we use in three other Get the Look posts.

2. The basket — ~$40

One large abaca or seagrass basket sits on the open shelf below. Holds 3-4 pairs of shoes (the everyday rotation; the rest go in the closet). Choose one with leather handles so you can pull it out. We use the GIB seagrass set or the happimess Laurel basket — both on Storage.

3. The lamp — ~$60

A small ceramic table lamp with a linen drum shade. Warm 2700K bulb, ideally on a smart plug or with a built-in dimmer. The lamp is the most important purchase in this room — it does more for the space at 7pm than any other piece. The PoKat small ceramic pair (~$60 for one of the two) is the easiest entry point.

4. The mirror — ~$45

Round, oak-framed, 24-30 inches diameter. Hangs above the console, centered. The mirror does two jobs: bounces the light from the lamp around the room, and gives you a glance-check on your way out the door. Skip rectangular and skip frameless — round + wood frame is the Japandi pattern.

5. The tray — ~$25

Small wood or stoneware tray, 8-10 inches across. Sits on the console next to the lamp. Catches keys, AirPods, the receipt from your jacket pocket. The Acacia wood serving tray we use throughout the brand works beautifully here. Browse on Ceramics & Tableware.

The math

Console $130 + basket $40 + lamp $60 + mirror $45 + tray $25 = $300. Add $10 for a small dried branch in whatever vase you already own. The room reads finished, intentional, and quietly Japandi from the moment you walk in.

What the room teaches you

An entryway done well sets the tone for the rest of the house. The eye that walks in expecting a pile of shoes and finds a styled console knows: this house is intentional. Whether the rest of the house is or isn't, the first impression has been made. It's the cheapest room to fix and the highest-leverage one to fix first.
