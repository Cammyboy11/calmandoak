The $500 Japandi dining table set

Most dining tables are styled twice a year (Thanksgiving, a birthday) and bare the rest of the time. The fix is a layered everyday setup that takes the table from 'where dinner happens' to 'where you want to sit'. Five pieces, $500 total.

The five pieces

1. The dinnerware (service for 6) — ~$160

An 18-piece stoneware dinnerware set in speckled cream or oat. Service for 6 (plate / bowl / dessert plate × 6). The AmorArc Stoneware Dinnerware (4.6★/1,533 reviews — Amazon's Choice, with the bonus of being a 4.5% commission category) on Ceramics & Tableware.

2. The linen tablecloth — ~$80

Cream heavyweight linen tablecloth (or a faux linen tablecloth — the Softalker Cream Faux Linen, 4.6★/784 reviews, holds up well). Softens the table, gives the eye a base layer.

3. The linen napkins (6) — ~$60

Six folded cream linen napkins, hemmed. Heavyweight, machine washable. The MLMW Beige Cream Napkins Set of 6 (4.6★/529 reviews) on Textiles.

4. The candle holder pair + tapers — ~$80

A pair of brass or matte black stoneware taper candle holders, with cream beeswax tapers. The Iron Taper Candle Holder Set of 3 (4.6★/250 reviews) is on Ceramics & Tableware.

5. The centerpiece vase + branch — ~$45

One short cream ribbed stoneware vase with one tall stem (eucalyptus, dried wheat, or pampas). The Vanselia Ceramic Vase (4.7★/809 reviews) on Ceramics & Tableware.

The remaining $75 of the budget covers a small wooden serving board (acacia, ~$30) and a stoneware salt cellar with wood lid (~$25), plus a pair of ceramic creamer/sugar vessels (~$20).

The styling order

Tablecloth down first. Plates, then bowls (stacked) at each setting. Linen napkin folded across each plate. Centerpiece vase + branch in the middle, two candle holders flanking. The salt cellar and the small board near one end of the table for serving.
