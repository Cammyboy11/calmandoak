The $400 small bedroom (under 150 sq ft)

Small bedrooms get harder to style than big ones because every piece has to do double duty. Five pieces, $400, and the room reads composed instead of cramped.

The five pieces (small-room edition)

1. The compact low oak bed — ~$200

A low-profile oak bed frame for a queen mattress. The lower the bed, the bigger the room reads (less visible volume above the bed = more apparent ceiling height). Browse Furniture for verified picks.

2. ONE nightstand (not a pair) — ~$80

In a small bedroom, a single nightstand on the side closer to the door. The other side gets a single sconce instead — frees up floor space, looks more intentional. Browse Bedroom.

3. The plug-in brass sconce — ~$45

A plug-in brass wall sconce on the wall opposite the nightstand (the side without the nightstand). No nightstand needed on that side.

The Dimmable Plug-in Wall Sconces Set of 2 (4.7★/569 reviews) on Lighting — buy the pair, use one on each side, OR use just one + a single nightstand on the other side.

4. One woven basket (replaces dresser) — ~$30

A large woven basket on the floor or in the corner replaces the dresser entirely in a small bedroom. Holds folded sweaters, throw blankets, the seasonal stuff that would otherwise sprawl. The happimess Laurel Abaca Basket (4.6★/99) on Storage.

5. The linen curtain panel — ~$45

A single linen-look curtain panel, 96 inches long. Hung high (close to the ceiling, not the window frame) to make the room feel taller. NICETOWN Natural Linen Curtains 84" (4.5★/742 reviews) on Textiles — get the longest length your wall allows.

The math

Bed $200 + nightstand $80 + sconce $45 + basket $30 + curtain $45 = $400.

Why these specific pieces in a small room

Each piece is doing double duty. The single nightstand frees floor space without sacrificing function (the sconce on the other side replaces it). The basket replaces a dresser. The low bed makes the ceiling feel higher. The long curtain hung high makes the wall feel taller.
