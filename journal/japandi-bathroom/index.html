The Japandi bathroom

The bathroom is the one room that's already close to a spa — it just needs decluttering and warming up. A handful of natural-material swaps turns the most utilitarian room in the house into the calmest.

The bathroom follows the same five principles — natural materials, restraint, warm light. Because it's small and mostly hard surfaces, a few soft, natural additions go a long way.

Storage in natural materials

Hide the plastic bottles and clutter behind natural-material storage: a slim bamboo shelf for folded towels, a lidded seagrass basket for the overflow, a small wood tray to corral what stays out. Warm materials soften a tiled room instantly — see the storage edit.

Switch to waffle linen towels

Nothing reads "spa" faster than a stack of waffle or linen towels in oatmeal or stone, replacing the mismatched bright ones. They dry quickly, get softer with washing, and look considered just folded on a shelf. Keep them to one colour family; the textiles edit has the set. (And here's how to keep linen at its best.)

Warm the light

Bright white bathroom lighting is the enemy of calm. If you can, switch to warm 2700K bulbs; if you can't change the fixture, a small candle or a warm plug-in light by the sink transforms an evening bath. Warm light makes every material look better.

A few quiet objects

Decant soap into a stoneware or glass bottle, add a small ceramic dish and a single stem or a sprig of eucalyptus. This is wabi-sabi at its most useful — handmade vessels that turn a routine into a ritual. Resist filling every surface; the empty space is the spa feeling.
