The $200 entryway organizer (small space)

Most apartments have no entryway — just a foot of wall by the front door. Five small pieces, $200 total, and even that single foot of wall becomes a styled landing zone.

The five pieces

1. The wall hooks (3) — ~$40

Three brass or oak wall hooks in a row. Mounted at varying heights. Hold coats, bags, dog leash. The variation in height is the styling — three hooks at the same height read like a coat closet; three hooks at staggered heights read like a vignette.

2. The small basket — ~$35

One round seagrass or abaca basket on the floor below the hooks. Holds shoes, package returns, the things that pile up. The happimess Laurel Abaca Basket (4.6★/99 reviews) on Storage.

3. The narrow shelf or floating ledge — ~$50

A small oak floating shelf, 18 inches wide. Holds keys, mail, a small ceramic bowl. The Fun Memories Oak Floating Shelves (4.6★/586 reviews) on Furniture — buy a single, not the pair.

4. The small mirror — ~$45

Round oak-framed mirror, 18-24 inches diameter. Hangs above the floating shelf. Doubles the visible space, gives you the glance-check on the way out.

5. The catch-all bowl — ~$25

Small stoneware bowl on the floating shelf. Catches keys, change, the small things. Browse Ceramics & Tableware.

The math

Hooks $40 + basket $35 + shelf $50 + mirror $45 + bowl $25 = $195. Even a one-foot wall becomes a styled landing zone.
