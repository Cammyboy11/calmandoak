The $250 Japandi bathroom

The bathroom is the cheapest room in any home to make Japandi. Most bathrooms are already neutral (white tile, white walls), and the upgrade is just textiles and a tray. Five purchases, under $250, and the bathroom shifts from rental to spa.

The five pieces

1. The towel set — ~$80

Cotton waffle weave bath towels in cream or oat. Lighter than terry, faster-drying, more visually interesting. A set of six (two bath, two hand, two washcloth) covers a household of two.

Verified pick: GILDEN TREE Waffle Towel Set (4.2★/295 reviews) on Textiles.

2. The bath mat — ~$45

Thick cotton ribbed bath mat in cream. Hotel-quality texture, machine washable. Browse Textiles for the verified picks.

3. The marble tray — ~$45

A small natural marble or travertine tray on the bathroom counter. Holds: a small ceramic soap dish, a glass apothecary jar, a small vase with one stem.

The Karibiber Luxury Marble Vanity Tray (4.6★/142 reviews) on Decor Accents.

4. The linen robe — ~$40

Cream linen waffle bathrobe, kimono cut. Lighter and faster-drying than terry. Hangs on a wooden hook on the back of the door (the robe is part of the styling when not in use).

5. The reed diffuser + small ceramic — ~$40

A hinoki or cedar reed diffuser in a small stoneware vessel on the counter. Plus a single small ceramic dish next to the sink for rings, watch, the small things.

Browse Outdoor & Wellness for diffuser picks.

The math

Towels $80 + mat $45 + tray $45 + robe $40 + diffuser/dish $40 = $250. The bathroom is now styled.

The optional sixth piece

For $30 more: a linen-look shower curtain. Replaces the plastic-vinyl one most rentals ship with. The Natural Linen Shower Curtain (4.4★/563 reviews) on Textiles.
