The $400 patio dining setup

Outdoor dining for two doesn't need a full patio set. Five pieces — a small teak table, two chairs, a runner, candleholders, a planter — turn a balcony or a back-porch corner into the dinner you'd photograph.

The five pieces

1. The teak table — ~$160

Solid teak or acacia outdoor table, 30-36 inches across. Big enough for two place settings + a centerpiece. Ages to silver-grey if you let it weather, or stays warm honey if you oil it annually.

The VICTORY RELAX Acacia Side Table (4.5★/459 reviews) on Outdoor & Wellness works perfectly.

2. Two outdoor chairs — ~$120 (pair)

Woven rattan or teak. Match the table material if possible. Browse Outdoor & Wellness for pairs.

3. The cordless lantern (or pair of lanterns) — ~$55

A cordless rechargeable lantern that sits in the middle of the table. Or a pair of smaller ones at each end. Set of 2 Cordless Table Lamps (4.5★/259 reviews).

4. The outdoor cushions / throw — ~$35

One pair of outdoor pillows in linen-look weatherproof fabric, on the chairs. Or one wool-look outdoor throw draped on the back of one chair.

5. The planter pair — ~$30

A pair of medium ceramic planters with herbs (rosemary, thyme) or a small olive tree. Sits on the floor flanking the table. Adds the green moment.

The math

Table $160 + chairs $120 + lantern $55 + cushions $35 + planters $30 = $400.

The dinner ritual

Once the patio is set up, dinner outside becomes weekly. Saturday at 6pm, the lantern goes on, the food comes out, the dinner takes 90 minutes instead of the indoor 30. The space is the prompt; the setup is what makes you actually use it.
