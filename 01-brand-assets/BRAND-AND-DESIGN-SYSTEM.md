# Calm & Oak — Brand & Design System

**Version:** 1.0 (locked 2026-05-08)
**Status:** Production-ready. All 6 pin templates have reference examples in `03-pin-templates/`. Use this as the canonical guide for every pin generated going forward.

---

## I. BRAND IDENTITY (CONDENSED)

**One line:** Curated finds for warm, calm, intentional homes. Quiet pieces that age well.

**The brand promise:** Magazine-quality editorial taste applied to affordable Amazon finds. We don't recommend anything we wouldn't own ourselves.

**Voice signature:**
- Editorial, slightly dry, never hyper or over-promising
- Builder-respectful — "you already know what you're doing, this is the system that catches up to you"
- Anti-jargon. No fluff. No exclamation marks
- "Curated" not "recommended" — implies taste

**Tone benchmark:** Kinfolk + Architectural Digest + Cereal magazine. NOT influencer-speak.

## II. VISUAL SYSTEM

### Color palette (use ONLY these — no exceptions)

| Role | Name | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary background | Off-white | `#F7F4EE` | Cream backgrounds, hook bands |
| Secondary background | Warm cream | `#EFE8DA` | Alternate, paper texture |
| Primary text | Charcoal | `#2A2A28` | Hooks, body text, brand wordmark |
| Subdued text | Soft graphite | `#5C5A55` | Sub-headlines, less important text |
| **Accent (THE accent)** | **Terracotta** | **`#C97B5C`** | Used sparingly — see "Accent rules" below |
| Accent alternate | Sand | `#D4B996` | Only if terracotta feels too warm — pick one and commit |

**Accent rules** (the brand voice depends on this):
- Terracotta appears on ~30-40% of pins, never all of them
- When used, it appears as ONE moment per pin: a horizontal rule, a number, a CTA, a small dot
- Never use terracotta and sand together — pick one for the entire brand
- Default: terracotta

### Typography

| Role | Typeface | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Hook text | Cormorant Garamond (or substitute serif) | Bold, all-caps, tracked slightly wide for hooks |
| Body text | Inter (or substitute sans) | Pin descriptions, sub-text |
| Brand wordmark | Cormorant Garamond | Mixed case "Calm & Oak", regular weight |

**Type rules:**
- Hooks are always serif, never sans
- Hooks use ALL-CAPS in 60% of pins, mixed-case in 40% (variation matters)
- Brand wordmark is always mixed case "Calm & Oak"
- Never use more than 2 typefaces on one pin

### Photography style

Every pin photograph must hit:
- **Soft natural light** (morning window, afternoon golden hour) — never harsh midday sun, never flash, never artificial-looking
- **Warm desaturated color grade** — slightly muted, never punchy or oversaturated
- **Natural materials only** — wood, linen, ceramic, stoneware, seagrass, jute, wool, paper, brass
- **Composition rule of three** — usually three objects max in a styled shot
- **No people, no faces** (avoids consent and platform issues)
- **No AI plastic glossy look** — must read as photographic, not 3D rendered
- **Magazine quality** — Kinfolk, Cereal, AD references

## III. THE SIX PIN TEMPLATES

Reference examples in `03-pin-templates/`. Every pin must use one of these templates.

### Template A — Hero (workhorse)

**File:** referenced by `04-sample-pins/day1-pin1-seagrass-baskets.png`

**Layout:**
- Top 22%: cream hook band with bold serif text, charcoal color, all-caps
- Middle 64%: hero product photograph with thin charcoal border
- Bottom 14%: cream band with brand wordmark in serif charcoal

**When to use:** Single-product pins. Direct, classic, the bread-and-butter.

**Frequency:** ~40% of pins

**Hook style:** Statement hooks ("THE LAMP THAT MAKES ANY ROOM EXPENSIVE")

**Accent usage:** Optional small terracotta rule under the brand wordmark

---

### Template B — Lifestyle Overlay

**File:** `03-pin-templates/template-B-lifestyle-overlay-example.png`

**Layout:**
- Full-bleed editorial photograph (no cream bands)
- Hook text overlaid on a darker zone of the image, in cream-white serif
- Small terracotta horizontal rule below the hook
- Brand wordmark in lower right corner, cream serif, 60% opacity

**When to use:** Moody/atmospheric pins. Evening lighting, intimate scenes, hero shots where the photo IS the message.

**Frequency:** ~20% of pins

**Hook style:** Atmospheric, mood-driven ("WHEN THE LIGHT GOES SOFT", "7 PM CALM")

**Accent usage:** ALWAYS — small terracotta rule under hook is the signature

**Critical:** Photo must have a darker zone where text can sit legibly. If the photo is too bright/busy, switch to Template A.

---

### Template C — Editorial Round-up

**File:** `03-pin-templates/template-C-editorial-roundup-example.png`

**Layout:**
- Top 18%: cream band with hook in serif charcoal, ending with terracotta rule
- Middle 64%: SINGLE editorial photograph composed as a still life of multiple products together (NOT a 2x2 grid)
- Bottom 18%: cream band with brand wordmark + small terracotta "TAP TO SHOP" CTA

**When to use:** Weekly round-up pins, "X products that work" listicle content.

**Frequency:** ~15% of pins (1 per week minimum, more during Week-1 launches and seasonal moments)

**Hook style:** Listicle ("3 STORAGE BASKETS WORTH KEEPING", "5 LAMPS FOR UNDER $80")

**Accent usage:** ALWAYS — terracotta rule + "TAP TO SHOP" CTA at bottom

**Critical:** The still life must look intentionally composed (Kinfolk-style), not catalog-flat. 3-5 products max in one scene. If you need to show more, generate two separate round-ups.

---

### Template D — Detail + Context Split

**File:** `03-pin-templates/template-D-detail-context-split-example.png`

**Layout:**
- Top 50%: tight macro detail shot (texture, weave, glaze, grain)
- Center 8% band: cream with hook in bold serif, optional terracotta dots/rule
- Bottom 42%: wider context shot showing the same product styled in a room
- Lower right corner: brand wordmark, subtle, 60% opacity

**When to use:** Texture-heavy products — ceramics, textiles, woven baskets, wood pieces. Anything where the material IS the selling point.

**Frequency:** ~10% of pins

**Hook style:** Material-evocative ("WABI-SABI, EVERYDAY", "WOVEN TO LAST", "HAND-THROWN, EVERYDAY USE")

**Accent usage:** Small terracotta dots or short rule in the center band

**Critical:** Both photos must show the SAME product. Don't pair a detail of one ceramic with a context shot of another — breaks trust.

---

### Template E — Numbered List (Kinfolk-style)

**File:** `03-pin-templates/template-E-numbered-list-example.png`

**Layout:**
- Generous cream background throughout
- Left third: ONE giant terracotta numeral in elegant serif, taking most of the vertical space
- Right two-thirds vertical layout:
  - Smaller serif headline ("WAYS TO STYLE")
  - Larger serif sub-headline ("AN OAK SHELF")
  - Thin charcoal horizontal rule
  - Single editorial photograph below the rule
- Bottom: brand wordmark centered, charcoal serif

**When to use:** Educational / styling content. "X ways to," "X mistakes to avoid," "X things every Y home needs."

**Frequency:** ~10% of pins

**Hook style:** Number-led ("3 WAYS TO STYLE AN OAK SHELF", "5 MISTAKES IN A SMALL BEDROOM", "7 CALM RITUALS")

**Accent usage:** ALWAYS — the giant terracotta numeral IS the accent

**Critical:** The numeral is the design element. Number must be legible, terracotta color, generous size (30-40% of left vertical). Don't crowd it with text.

---

### Template F — Get the Look

**File:** `03-pin-templates/template-F-get-the-look-example.png`

**Layout:**
- Top 14%: cream band with "GET THE LOOK" + sub-headline, terracotta rule beneath
- Middle 76%: single magazine-quality room photograph with subtle terracotta numbered circles (1, 2, 3, 4) overlaid near key products
- Bottom 10%: cream band with brand wordmark + small terracotta "TAP TO SHOP EACH PIECE" CTA

**When to use:** Whole-room aspirational pins. The highest-converting affiliate format because buyers can shop multiple products from one pin.

**Frequency:** ~5% of pins (1-2 per week)

**Hook style:** Always "GET THE LOOK: A [room descriptor]" ("A QUIET LIVING ROOM", "A WARM JAPANDI BEDROOM", "A SLOW SUNDAY KITCHEN")

**Accent usage:** ALWAYS — terracotta numbered circles + bottom CTA

**Critical:** Each numbered product must correspond to a real Amazon affiliate link in the pin description (numbered 1-4 in the description matching the photo).

## V. HOOK FRAMEWORK

Hook style varies across pins to avoid visual fatigue. Use this rotation:

### Hook category 1: Statement (40% of hooks)
"THE LAMP THAT MAKES ANY ROOM EXPENSIVE"
"THE LINEN BEDDING WORTH THE SPLURGE"
"HIDE THE CHAOS, KEEP THE CALM"

### Hook category 2: Numbered (20%)
"3 STORAGE BASKETS WORTH KEEPING"
"5 LAMPS UNDER $80"
"7 CALM SUNDAY RITUALS"

### Hook category 3: Atmospheric / Mood (15%)
"WHEN THE LIGHT GOES SOFT"
"7 PM IN A JAPANDI HOME"
"SUNDAY MORNING CALM"

### Hook category 4: Question (10%)
"TIRED OF PLASTIC BINS?"
"WHY DOES YOUR BEDROOM FEEL OFF?"
"WHAT MAKES A ROOM CALM?"

### Hook category 5: Story / First-person (10%)
"I TESTED 5 — HERE'S THE WINNER"
"I BOUGHT THIS TWICE"
"THE LAMP I'D BUY AGAIN"

### Hook category 6: Anti / Hot take (5%)
"STOP USING IKEA DISHES"
"DONATE THE PLASTIC"
"SKIP OVERHEAD LIGHTS FOREVER"

**Word-level rules:**
- Avoid: "ULTIMATE", "BEST", "AMAZING", "GAME-CHANGING", exclamation marks, emoji
- Prefer: specific objects ("THE LAMP" not "the lighting"), specific outcomes ("EXPENSIVE" not "elevated"), small numbers (3-7 work best)

## VI. PIN DESCRIPTION SEO RULES

Pinterest reads pin descriptions for search ranking. Optimize as follows.

### Structure (in order)
1. **First line (≤100 chars):** Hook restated + primary keyword. This is what Pinterest's algo weighs most.
2. **Body (200-400 chars):** Description of product + scene + secondary keywords. Conversational but keyword-rich.
3. **Hashtags:** 5-7 tags, mix of broad (#japandi, #homedecor) + niche (#warmminimalism, #intentionalliving) + product-specific (#bedsidelamp, #seagrassbasket).
4. **Affiliate disclosure (last line):** "Affiliate link — I earn a small commission if you buy through this link, at no extra cost to you."

### Keyword density
- Primary keyword (e.g., "japandi") appears 2-3 times naturally
- Brand promise words: "calm", "intentional", "warm", "minimal", "natural materials"
- Avoid keyword stuffing (Pinterest demotes it)

### Length
- Sweet spot: 250-450 characters
- Never under 150 (Pinterest's algo deprioritizes thin descriptions)
- Never over 500 (truncates in feed)

### Move disclosure to LAST line
Earlier docs had disclosure first. **Update:** disclosure goes last so the first 100 chars (which Pinterest weights heavily) carry hook + keyword instead.

## VII. QUALITY CHECKLIST (BEFORE EVERY PUBLISH)

Run this list for every single pin before scheduling in Blotato:

### Visual
- [ ] Image is 1000×1500 (2:3 ratio)
- [ ] Color palette is on-brand (cream/charcoal/terracotta only)
- [ ] Typography is serif for hook, mixed-case for brand wordmark
- [ ] Photography is editorial-quality, no AI plastic look
- [ ] Hook is readable at thumbnail size (squint test)
- [ ] Brand wordmark is present and consistent
- [ ] Template chosen matches content type (per Section IV)

### Text
- [ ] No typos in hook
- [ ] No typos in description
- [ ] No emoji or exclamation marks (unless deliberate for the format)
- [ ] No "AI tells" in copy ("delve", "elevate", "in the realm of", "transformative")

### SEO
- [ ] Description is 250-450 chars
- [ ] First 100 chars contain primary keyword
- [ ] 5-7 hashtags present
- [ ] Affiliate disclosure on last line

### Affiliate
- [ ] Real Amazon affiliate URL (via SiteStripe), not raw amazon.com link
- [ ] Tested in incognito window (link redirects through tracking ID)
- [ ] Product is in stock
- [ ] Product matches the visual aesthetic (not just metrics)

### Distribution
- [ ] Target board chosen (per pillar mapping)
- [ ] Posting time slotted (8am / 12pm / 3pm / 7pm / 10pm rotation)
- [ ] Template pattern: not the same template as the previous pin posted

If any checkbox is unchecked, fix before publishing.

## VIII. WHAT MAGAZINE-QUALITY MEANS HERE

When in doubt, ask: **"Would this pin fit on the Pinterest of Apartment Therapy or Domino magazine?"**

If yes — ship it.
If no — fix the photography, the hook, or the layout.

We are not racing to the bottom on price-led affiliate content. We are building a brand that buyers trust because every pin earns their saved-folder space. Volume comes from quality compounding over months — not from cranking out mediocre pins.

**The standard is incredible. Don't sacrifice it.**

---

## IX. DELIVERY PIPELINE FOR EVERY NEW PIN

For every new pin going forward:

1. **Pick the template** (per Section IV rotation)
2. **Write the hook** (per Section V categories — vary from previous pin)
3. **Generate the image** via the Node helper at `/tmp/genimg.js` with a prompt that explicitly references the template specs in this document
4. **Write the description** (per Section VI rules)
5. **Run the quality checklist** (Section VII)
6. **Schedule in Blotato** with all metadata (board, time, link)

Every step in this pipeline is the same for every pin. The variation comes from template choice + hook angle + product. The brand consistency comes from never deviating from the system.
