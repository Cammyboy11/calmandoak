# The Calm & Oak Edit — Outreach Plan & Target List

**Goal:** get Volume One in front of people who already have a warm, aligned audience — and convert that into shares, features, and ongoing referrals. Honest from the start: we send a genuine free copy and ask for an honest look. No paid fake reviews, no invented endorsements.

**What we're sending:** a free comp of the **complete Volume One** (the $39 bundle — give them code `VOLUMEONE` so it's free, or just attach the PDFs), or point them to the genuinely-free **Issue One — Winter** (`payhip.com/b/REM9Q`).

**Our single best hook:** the **72 Japanese microseasons** structure. It's not "another Japandi shop guide" — it's a seasonal almanac. That angle lands hardest with slow-living and seasonal-calendar audiences, who are exactly the people most likely to share a beautiful quarterly journal.

## How to run it (simple sequence)

1. **Enable Payhip affiliates** on the bundle (one-time, ~5 min).
2. **Week 1:** Tier 1 — 8–10 personal messages (newsletters reply fastest). Mine the Splendid to Be directory for 15–20 more.
3. **Week 2:** Tier 2 Instagram DMs + Tier 3 publication pitches.
4. **Track** in a simple sheet: name · platform · link · contact · date sent · reply · outcome. (I can build this as a spreadsheet.)
5. **Follow up once** after ~7 days if no reply, then leave it.

**Realistic expectation:** Tier 1 newsletters are where the first wins come from — a single feature in an aligned slow-living newsletter can outsell a 600K Instagram story, because the audience is there to *read seasonal things*, not scroll.
