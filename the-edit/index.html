What's inside an issue

Not a catalogue. A way of paying attention.

The almanac

Each issue opens on the season's microseasons — the five-day weeks of the old Japanese calendar — and what they ask of a home.

The room

One room, fully realised, then sourced piece by piece: the aesthetic first, the price second, every find affiliate-linked.

The lesson

A single styling principle from the seven of Zen aesthetics — seijaku, kanso, ma, wabi-sabi — made practical.

The ritual

A small seasonal practice — the four-o'clock fire, one branch of ikebana, the long breakfast, the gathering.

The edit

Twelve to fifteen considered things per issue, grouped the way a room is actually built. Buy slowly, buy once.
