## 3. Shop story — the About page essay

This goes in the "Story" field of the About page. Aim is ~250–280 words across 5 short paragraphs. Voice: editorial, slightly dry, first-person, no exclamation marks.

### Final version (paste this)

```
Calm & Oak makes prints for rooms that don't shout.

I started this studio because I wanted art that earned its wall space —
pieces that hold a room rather than fill it. The aesthetic is Japandi:
the Japanese discipline of negative space and *ma* (the considered pause)
meeting the Scandinavian respect for natural materials and quiet color.
None of it is loud. All of it is intentional.

The collection runs across six series. **Sumi-e ink** — single-breath
brushstrokes in the Japanese tradition, with a vermillion hanko seal.
**Botanical line** — one continuous ink line for a stem, an olive branch,
a ginkgo leaf. **Minimalist landscape** — a soft horizon, a moon, a lone
tree. **Japanese calligraphy** — Shizuka, Wa, Ma. **Tonal study** — oak
and walnut, sage and stone. And **wabi-sabi still life** — three stones,
a tea ceremony, light through a linen curtain.

Every print is hand-illustrated and produced on 250 gsm museum-quality
matte paper — acid-free, FSC-certified, archival inks. Printed in the
US and shipped flat. The paper is warm off-white, not bright white —
chosen because Japandi rooms don't tolerate harsh contrasts.

Each piece stands alone or pairs into a gallery wall set. The set
listings ship the three prints designed to balance together, at a saving
over buying individually.

If you have a question about a piece, the paper, or a custom size —
message me. I read everything.

— Calm & Oak
```

(264 words — Etsy About allows up to 5,000 chars; this is ~1,500.)

### When you have time, replace with real photography

These cards are honest brand graphics, but **authentic photos convert ~30–60% better on the About page** ([Etsy seller handbook](https://www.etsy.com/seller-handbook/article/22636178725)). When you have an hour with an iPhone, shoot:

1. **Studio flat-lay** — your real workspace, brushes, paper samples, one dried branch. Top-down, natural window light, no flash.
2. **Print being packaged** — a single print being slid into a kraft tube or flat mailer with the brand stamp visible.
3. **Finished gallery wall in situ** — three prints framed on a wall in a real room (yours, a friend's, anywhere lived-in).
4. **Hands holding a print** — hands at work, or hands inspecting a finished print at golden hour.
5. **Detail shot** — close-up of paper edge + ink, raking side-light to show the 250 gsm tooth.

Replace each card 1-for-1 as you shoot. Maintain the same dimensions (760×468) and the same warm-natural-light color grade.

## 5. Featured video — the storyboard

Etsy allows one short video (5–15 sec on About page, MP4, <100 MB). It autoplays muted on the About page, so make it work without sound.

I can't film a video for you — that needs a camera in a real space. Here's the **30-second iPhone shoot brief** so you can knock it out in an hour:

### Brief: "A Quiet Studio" — 15 seconds

**Setting:** your workspace (table, light source, a few props). Morning or afternoon window light only. No overhead lighting, no flash.

**Shots (3 sec each, 5 shots total):**

1. **Open** — slow vertical pan from sumi-e ink bowl up to a blank sheet of paper on the table.
2. **Hand and brush** — hand picking up the brush, dipping into the ink (no face).
3. **The mark** — close-up overhead of a single brushstroke being made on the paper. (Use a print you've already made, or do this once for real — even one take.)
4. **The finished print** — pan back to reveal the finished piece on the table, oak frame leaning beside it.
5. **The wall** — final shot: the print framed on the wall, soft natural light, hold for 2 sec.

**Tone:** zero music (autoplays muted anyway), zero text overlays, zero cuts that feel rushed. Each shot holds for ~3 sec. The pace is "Kinfolk video," not "TikTok."

**Tech:**
- Shoot 4K vertical (9:16) on iPhone with "Cinematic" mode off (Cinematic adds fake blur)
- Export at 1080p MP4
- Trim in iMovie or just iPhone Photos
- Target file size: 30–60 MB

### Placeholder option

If you want a video LIVE on day one before you shoot, tell me — I can generate a 15-second slideshow of your prints with brand-graphic title cards. It'll look intentional but it won't have the "real studio" warmth. Most premium Etsy shops skip the video at launch and add it in week 2–3 once they have time to shoot.
