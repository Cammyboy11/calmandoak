## A5. Tips for highest conversions

1. **Reuse validated ASINs.** When a product earns its way into the cheat sheet, ride that horse. Don't search for a new "wool throw" if `B08F48LR45` is already great — link it again with different copy.

2. **Round-up pins (Sundays) are conversion gold.** They bundle multiple products in one click-target. Either link to the highest-priced product OR — better — link the round-up pin to your `/shop/[pillar]/` page where the visitor sees all 5 in one scrollable layout.

3. **Hooks > products.** Pinterest users save the *aesthetic*, not the product. The hook (CAPS first line) is what makes someone save. The product URL is what monetizes the saver later.

4. **Vary hashtags.** Pinterest penalizes identical 5-tag sets across consecutive pins. Use the hashtag pools at the top of this doc; rotate so no two pins in the same week share more than 2 tags.

5. **The 3-day anchor.** Pinterest gives new pins their biggest push in the first 72 hours. Batch-schedule on Sunday so Monday's 5 pins all go out fresh — gives the algorithm a "creator is active" signal.

6. **Refresh your top performers.** After 60 days, look at Pinterest analytics. Take your top 5 highest-saved pins and create a new pin for each (different image, different hook, same product URL). Pinterest treats this as fresh content; you re-ride the algo wave.

7. **Convert search URLs over time.** As you schedule each 🔍 SEARCH pin, the ASIN you pick should be added to the cheat sheet (A1) and reused in later pins. After Day 42, you should have ~80+ verified ASINs. Update this doc as you go.

8. **Watch for "frequently returned" warnings.** Amazon flags products with high return rates and reverses your commission. The VASAGLE cabinet (Pin 12) is currently flagged. Check each ASIN page for this banner before committing.
