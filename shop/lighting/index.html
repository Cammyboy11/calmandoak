How to choose Japandi lighting

Lighting does more for a room's mood than any other purchase — and it's where most homes go wrong. One bright ceiling light flattens everything.

Warm and dimmable

Choose 2700K bulbs (look for "warm white") and put them on dimmers. Cool white kills the calm instantly. This is the whole idea behind lighting the 5pm room.

Layers, not one source

Aim for three pools of light at different heights — a floor lamp, a table lamp, a wall sconce — instead of one overhead. The room gains depth and you can turn the day down gradually.

Natural shades

Paper, rattan, linen, and bamboo shades diffuse light softly and add texture even when switched off. Skip bare bulbs, cool-white LEDs, and relying on the ceiling fixture alone.

Light first, then everything else.

The single fastest Japandi upgrade in any room: replace one bright overhead bulb with a paper pendant, and replace one cool-white bulb anywhere with a 2700K warm-white. Do those two things and the room is already 60% there.

Avoid: cool-white LEDs (3500K and up), exposed Edison bulbs, anything described as "industrial." Japandi light is paper-soft, not gallery-bright.
