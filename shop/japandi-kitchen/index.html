How to choose for a Japandi kitchen

A Japandi kitchen is about what you take off the counter, not what you add. The goal is calm at the busiest surface in the house.

Clear the counter

Corral the daily clutter onto one wood tray and put everything else away — a single tray instantly makes a counter look composed. Empty surface is the luxury.

Wood and stoneware over plastic

Acacia boards, a stoneware crock for utensils, linen tea towels. These materials are out on display anyway, so make them the ones worth seeing.

Edit the open shelf

If you have open shelving, show a restrained set of matching stoneware, not the whole cupboard. Skip branded plastic and single-use gadgets that live on the counter.

Stop using IKEA dishes.

The single biggest tell that a kitchen isn't Japandi: white-glossy round dishes. Swap them for matte, slightly speckled stoneware in a soft cream or sand. Same money, complete tonal shift.

Then: hide the plastic. Decant your dishwasher tablets, your cling film, your vitamins, your everything-with-a-loud-label into stoneware canisters or seagrass baskets. The counter calms down immediately.
