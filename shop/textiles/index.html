How to choose Japandi textiles

Textiles are what stop a minimal room feeling cold. Get the fibre and the tone right and a bare room turns warm without adding any clutter.

Natural fibres, always

Linen, wool, cotton, and jute breathe, soften, and look better with age — the opposite of polyester, which pills and stays flat. If you learn one distinction, learn when to use linen, cotton, or wool.

Tone on tone

Keep everything in one colour family — oat, sand, stone, cream — and let texture do the contrast instead of colour. A waffle weave beside a chunky knit beside flat linen reads rich; three colours reads busy. More on the Japandi palette.

Get the rug size right

The most common mistake is a rug that's too small. As a rule the front legs of your furniture should sit on it — see how to size a rug. Skip synthetic velvet and busy prints.

Layer like a magazine, not a catalog.

The trick: every textile in the room must feel different in your hand. Heavy linen on the bed, lighter linen at the windows, wool on the seat, jute under the foot. Same neutral palette, four different textures. That contrast is what makes a Japandi room feel three-dimensional in photos.

Skip the cushion sets that come matched. Buy three different cushions, in three different weaves, in the same colour family.
