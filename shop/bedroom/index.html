How to choose Japandi bedroom pieces

The bedroom is the easiest room to get right and the one where calm matters most. Three things carry it.

Low bed, natural bedding

A low platform bed and washed-linen bedding do most of the work. Linen looks better rumpled than cotton looks pressed — and it lasts, if you know how to care for it.

Symmetry at the bedside

A matched pair of nightstands and lamps reads intentional where one of each reads accidental. More on styling a nightstand.

Keep the palette quiet

One restful colour family, nothing loud near the bed. See it pulled together in a small bedroom for $400. Skip glossy synthetic headboards and bright accent colours.

A bedroom built to breathe

A Japandi bedroom is mostly negative space. Keep the bed low, the bedding heavy, and the surfaces almost empty. One reading light. One small ceramic vessel. A single piece of art at eye level when seated.

If you're starting from zero, prioritise in this order: bedding (changes the room overnight), bed frame (low, wood), one good lamp, then everything else.
