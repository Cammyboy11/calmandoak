Ensō

The incomplete circle — wabi-sabi's quietest, most famous mark.

The story

The ensō is the single most recognisable mark in Zen art — a circle drawn in one breath, with one brush, and never corrected. Ours is left open on purpose; the gap is the whole point. A closed circle is a statement, an open one an invitation.

It asks for space rather than company. Hang it alone on a wide, calm wall, or above a low oak console with a single stoneware vessel beneath — the one piece in the room that asks you to slow down.
