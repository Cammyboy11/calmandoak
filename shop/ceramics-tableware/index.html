Three, never four.

A Japandi tablescape works on the rule of three: one tall (vase or pitcher), one wide (bowl), one small (cellar, lidded vessel). Add a fourth and the table starts to feel "set." The whole point is that it shouldn't.

Speckled, matte, slightly imperfect glaze beats glossy and uniform every time. The eye reads the variation as hand-made, even when it's mass-produced.
