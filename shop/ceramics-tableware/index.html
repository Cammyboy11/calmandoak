How to choose Japandi ceramics

Ceramics are where Japandi's wabi-sabi heart shows. The pieces should feel made by a hand, not a machine.

Embrace the imperfect

A slightly uneven rim, a speckled glaze, a matte finish — these are features, not flaws. That's the whole spirit of wabi-sabi ceramics.

Earthy, matte, muted

Choose oatmeal, clay, charcoal, and off-white over bright glazes. Matte and speckled finishes catch light softly and sit happily with wood and linen.

Odd numbers, varied heights

Group vases and vessels in threes at different heights, each with a single stem — the one-stem rule always works. Skip glossy matched sets and bright patterns.

Three, never four.

A Japandi tablescape works on the rule of three: one tall (vase or pitcher), one wide (bowl), one small (cellar, lidded vessel). Add a fourth and the table starts to feel "set." The whole point is that it shouldn't.

Speckled, matte, slightly imperfect glaze beats glossy and uniform every time. The eye reads the variation as hand-made, even when it's mass-produced.
