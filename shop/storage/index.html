How to choose Japandi storage

In a Japandi home, storage's job is to disappear. The goal isn't more containers — it's surfaces that stay clear.

Closed beats open

Hide the visual noise behind doors, lids, and lined baskets; save open shelving for the few things worth looking at. The test: could a guest open this room and see nothing that needs tidying? More on storage that doesn't look like storage.

Natural materials

Seagrass, rattan, bamboo, and oak read as part of the room, not as utility. They soften a corner the way a plastic bin never will.

One job each

Buy storage for a specific thing — the throw, the shoes, the cables — not "general clutter." Vague storage just relocates mess. Skip wire racks and stackable plastic.

The disappearance test.

Good Japandi storage passes one test: from across the room, you should not see "storage." You should see a basket, a wooden box, a slatted cabinet — objects that earn their place visually before they earn it functionally.

Plastic bins fail this test. Bright-coloured organisers fail this test. Anything labelled "modular" usually fails it too.

Buy fewer, bigger, more beautiful storage pieces. They cost more once and disappear forever.
