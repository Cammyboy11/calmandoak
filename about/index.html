About Calm & Oak

A quiet home, slowly built.

Calm & Oak began on Pinterest — quiet rooms, warm wood, the kind of homes that feel breathed-in rather than decorated. This is the same idea, but with every piece tagged, vetted, and photographed honestly, so you can actually live in it.

Most “shop the look” content points you at pieces that are sold out, mislabelled, or nothing like the photo. So we built the resource we wished existed — a calm catalogue where the picture is the real product, the link actually works, and someone has already asked the only questions that matter:

Would I put it in my own home? Could a renter use it? Will it still look right in five years?

If a piece can’t answer all three, it doesn’t make the cut. What’s left is a small, honest edit of things worth living with — and the room-by-room guides we wished we’d had when we were making a Japandi home on a real budget.

The promise

How every piece earns its place

01

Vetted, never scraped

Every product is checked by hand — in stock, genuinely well-reviewed, and not quietly returned by half the people who bought it. No auto-generated lists, no filler.

02

The real thing, made beautiful

The photo on every card is the actual product, re-shot in our own light and palette — so what lands on your doorstep is what you saw. No borrowed studio shots, no bait-and-switch.

03

Honest about what works

The guides are specific and practical — including what we ruled out and why. If something only works in a magazine, we say so.

The aesthetic

What Japandi means here

Less a trend, more a rule of thumb: rooms that feel quiet. Wood you’d want to touch. Bedding that improves with age. Lighting that reads warm, never bright. One restrained colour, never two. Everything else, gently removed. It’s where Japanese restraint and Scandinavian warmth meet — the calm of both, the clutter of neither.

Who’s behind it

The person doing the vetting

Cameron Hayes

Founder & Editor

I came to Japandi from both directions. I lived in Denmark for eight years — long enough for Scandinavian design to stop being a style and start being how a home simply feels — and I travel to Japan whenever I can, where I fell for the restraint, the craft, and the way a room can hold almost nothing and still feel complete. Calm & Oak is where those two halves meet.

I read every review, open every product, and write every guide. When I tell you a piece is worth it, it’s because it passed my own three questions first.

— Cameron
